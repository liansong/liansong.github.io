Morning Mates

This was a project initially conceived for a design challenge as a part of my application to Berkeley Innovation. The prompt was as follows:

Consider the experience of waking up. Define a user problem and propose a design solution.

My initial submission consisted of a set of powerpoint slides, shown here, but I thought it would be a good exercise to follow through and take my original proposal through the iterative design process.

The Problem

I am terrible at waking up. I’ve tried everything — my typical alarm requires two digit multiplication to shut off, and it vibrates like mad. As much as a value punctuality, it’s become second nature to shut off my alarm(s) and simply fall back asleep.

That’s a big problem.

So when I was presented with this design challenge, I decided to approach it by first considering the perspective of the user — in this case, me.

What I realized was that this wasn’t an alarm-side issue; rather, it was a matter of motivation. I noticed, for instance, that I was very capable of waking up for morning appointments and tests — the fear of missing social obligations was enough to make me heed my alarm. Indeed, it had become a sort of a habit to ask a friend to call me in the morning and make sure I was awake, because knowing there was someone on the other end of the line sufficient social motivation.

The Initial Solution

“Social”, I noticed, was the key word in these early considerations. Moving forward, what I hoped to do was communalize the morning process, by creating a local network to hold one another accountable. My solution would be a mobile application, and my target audience would be a college floor, similar the one I currently live in. A bit of a niche application, sure, but I thought it would be fun to make it anyways.

Ultimately, I settled on two primary functionalities for this application:

  1. Calling Companions — match morning schedules with people on your floor, and hold each other accountable based on these matches.

  2. Breakfast Buddies — plan and eat breakfast with floormates; nothing starts your morning like a good breakfast with friends!
